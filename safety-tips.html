Fire Safety Tips

Oven Fires

Do not open the oven door if you see a fire raging on the inside of it.

Turn off the oven and let it cool down as much as possible when a fire is inside it.

Use a Class A fire extinguisher, a handy tool to put up the fire.

Microwave Fires

Do not use metal, metal edged bowls, metal foil or even twist ties in microwave ovens.

Turn off the microwave and let it cool down as much as possible when a fire is inside it.

If the fire continues once you’ve closed the door, contact the fire department.

Kitchen Fires

Do not use handphone, when the stove is turned on in the kitchen.

If there is a fire, turn off the stove and use a fire extinguisher to put up the fire.

If the fire continues once you’ve closed the door, contact the fire department.

Room Fires

Do not let children play with fire in a hidden place – such as a bedroom or closet.

Most fires started by children occur when they are left at home alone or unsupervised.

Install smoke alarms on every level of the house, outside all sleeping areas, and inside each bedroom.

Plug Fires

Never overload any extension cords. Install additional outlets if you need more of them

Wiring problems are often overlooked, creating a major fire threat. Keep an eye out for the outdated wiring.

If there is an electrical fire, pull the plug out, or switch off the power at the fuse box.
